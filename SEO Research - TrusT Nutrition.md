# SEO Research - TrusT Nutrition

Date: 2026-03-27

This note captures the live SEO research completed for TrusT Nutrition / TrusT Nutrition and Movement. It excludes later homepage rewrite recommendations and is intended as a reference brief for positioning, content, site structure, and implementation planning.

## Business Context

- Brand: TrusT Nutrition / TrusT Nutrition and Movement
- Location: Christchurch, New Zealand
- Delivery: In-person in Christchurch CBD and online
- Main CTA: free 15-minute chat / complimentary call, booked through Setmore
- Core positioning: personalised nutrition guidance for adults wanting to improve health, manage weight, build strength, better manage chronic conditions, and keep doing the things that matter most
- Vision: TrusT Nutrition and Movement helps adults living with chronic illness return to the activities that matter most
- Mission: TrusT Nutrition and Movement supports adults living with chronic illness through personalised nutrition guidance and tailored exercise support, working alongside them to strengthen mobility, independence, and confidence
- Important nuance: chronic illness is a differentiator, but the brand should still appeal to broader clients wanting weight loss, muscle gain, better energy, or general wellbeing
- Constraint: do not overstate qualifications or imply exercise/clinical claims beyond current credentials

## Executive Summary

### Core finding

The best SEO growth path is not to position the site as an arthritis-only business. The strongest opportunity is a hybrid model:

- Primary SEO position: Christchurch nutritionist
- Secondary primary SEO position: online nutrition consultations NZ
- Supporting commercial service themes: weight management, energy, general wellbeing, gut health
- Differentiator: arthritis, inflammation, and broader chronic condition support

### Why

Live SERPs favoured:

- local practitioner/service pages for Christchurch terms
- broad online consultation pages for NZ-wide terms
- niche condition pages as supporting assets rather than the homepage core

### Biggest constraints

- Branded search collision with `trustnutrition.com`, a supplement retailer
- The homepage/title positioning observed in search was still arthritis-led rather than Christchurch-led
- `Dietitian` is a protected title in New Zealand, so the brand should stay clearly positioned as a nutritionist / Associate Registered Nutritionist unless qualifications change
- Some currently indexed package/store URLs appear thin compared with stronger service-led pages

## Search Landscape Summary

### Highest-value themes

1. Christchurch nutritionist / local service intent
2. Online nutritionist NZ / national service intent
3. Weight management and body composition support
4. Chronic condition support, especially arthritis/inflammation
5. Energy, healthy ageing, bone health, and lifestyle support

### SERP behaviour observed

Google appears to reward:

- local practitioner pages for city-led searches
- broad service pages for online/national searches
- trust-building pages such as FAQs and about pages
- condition/topic pages when tightly aligned to a practitioner niche

Less likely to perform well:

- vague homepage copy with no local target
- thin product/package pages with little unique content
- over-narrow homepage positioning unless the business wants to commit to a single-condition niche

### Competitor pattern

The strongest competing sites generally do at least one of these things well:

- target a city clearly
- target a service clearly
- target a niche clearly
- reinforce trust with qualifications, FAQs, proof, and consultation details

TrusT Nutrition can win by combining:

- Christchurch local relevance
- strong online NZ accessibility
- practical, non-restrictive health support
- a visible but not overbearing chronic-condition differentiator

## Positioning Recommendation

### Recommended positioning model

Use a hybrid model:

- Homepage: Christchurch nutritionist plus online support
- Core service pages: weight management, online consultations, chronic condition support, gut health, healthy ageing / bone health
- Supporting niche page: arthritis and inflammation support

### Recommended message hierarchy

1. Local and practical nutrition support
2. In-person Christchurch + online NZ access
3. Help with weight, energy, strength, and long-term wellbeing
4. Special interest in chronic conditions such as arthritis and inflammation
5. Personalised support and clear next steps

### Brand naming recommendation

- Use `TrusT Nutrition` as the primary SEO brand name
- Use `TrusT Nutrition and Movement` as a supporting descriptor or future-facing secondary brand line
- Avoid letting the longer name dominate titles and H1s unless movement support is clearly scoped and qualification-safe

### Why this matters

There is branded search noise around `trustnutrition.com`, so SEO pages need stronger non-brand intent signals in titles, H1s, and copy.

## On-Page SEO Recommendations

### Titles and headings

- Move the homepage away from arthritis-first titling
- Lead with Christchurch and online availability where relevant
- Use one clear H1 per page
- Match page titles to commercial search intent rather than aspirational language alone

### Copy structure

- Homepage should balance weight, energy, general wellbeing, and chronic-condition support
- Each service page should have:
  - a clear target problem
  - who it is for
  - how support works
  - FAQs
  - trust/proof
  - CTA

### Internal linking

Prioritise links between:

- homepage
- consultations
- free call page
- weight management
- chronic conditions
- gut health
- healthy ageing / bone health
- relevant blog posts

### Trust and E-E-A-T

Add or strengthen:

- qualification wording that is accurate and specific
- professional memberships/registration if applicable
- consultation process clarity
- testimonials linked to problem types where appropriate
- clear policies: terms, cancellations, what to expect, privacy
- local identity: Christchurch CBD, online across NZ

## Local SEO Recommendations

### Google Business Profile

Optimise or create a strong Christchurch listing with:

- exact business name used consistently
- correct primary category
- consultation hours
- booking link
- service descriptions
- real photos
- Christchurch CBD service area/location details as appropriate

### Citations and directories

Recommended citation opportunities:

- Nutrition Society of New Zealand directory
- Healthpages
- Apple Business Connect
- Bing Places
- other credible NZ practitioner directories where a nutritionist listing is appropriate

### Reviews

Request reviews consistently after meaningful client progress points. Encourage natural mention of:

- Christchurch

- gut health
- arthritis/inflammation

### Local landing page guidance

One strong Christchurch page is preferable to weak suburb doorway pages unless there is real location-specific differentiation.

## Technical and Schema Recommendations

### Technical priorities

- Ensure unique title tags and meta descriptions across core pages
- Review indexation and canonical handling for package/store pages
- Confirm important pages are crawlable and linked from navigation
- Create or validate sitemap and robots directives
- Make sure the blog hub is actually indexable and useful before expanding content

### Schema opportunities

- `ProfessionalService` or `LocalBusiness`
- `Person`
- `Service`
- `FAQPage`
- review/testimonial schema only where compliant and clearly visible on-page

### AI visibility note

Clear structure, explicit service pages, strong FAQs, and credible trust signals will help both search and AI-assisted discovery.

## Conversion and Trust Recommendations

### Improve conversion flow

- Keep the free 15-minute chat highly visible
- Add a clear “what happens after booking” page
- Explain process, timing, and next steps
- Surface terms/cancellation expectations before purchase
- Use proof and testimonials more specifically

### Qualification-safe wording

Use accurate phrasing such as:

- personalised nutrition support
- practical nutrition guidance
- support for adults living with chronic conditions
- evidence-informed approach

Avoid overclaiming:

- treatment claims
- medical language that implies dietetic or clinical authority not currently held
- movement/exercise claims that exceed current qualification scope

## Final Strategic Recommendation

Do not make the homepage a chronic-illness-only page.

Instead:

- use Christchurch nutritionist and online consultations as the broad market-facing SEO foundation
- use weight, energy, and wellbeing to widen appeal
- use arthritis, inflammation, and chronic-condition support as the distinctive special-interest layer

This structure gives TrusT Nutrition the best chance to:

- rank locally
- attract online clients nationally
- keep the brand commercially broad
- still own a meaningful niche over time
